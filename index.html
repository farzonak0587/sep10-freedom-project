Biology in the Tech Age

Biology is all around us and has always been a huge part of science. But in recent years, technology has totally changed the way we learn about it and use it in real life. From apps that make studying fun to cool machines that help doctors see inside our bodies, there are so many tech tools that make biology more exciting and easier to understand. iXplain GCSE Biology App

Purpose: A fun and engaging way to learn biology, especially for students studying for their GCSE Biology exams.

Features:

  • Breaks down content unit by unit, making it easy to start from scratch.
  • Keep learning interactive and engaging.
  • Perfect for students preparing for exams.

Microbiology App

Purpose: Helps you study microbiology in depth.

Features:

  • Includes 58 study lessons, 26 quizzes, and 439 practice questions.
  • Organized into 26 units covering topics such as acellular pathogens, microbial growth, and infections.
  • Free to use and excellent for exam preparation.

3D Biology+ App

Purpose: Explore biological structures in 3D for a more interactive study experience.

Features:

  • Visualize biological items and structures (like cells and proteins) in 3D.
  • Allows you to change perspectives for a deeper understanding.
  • Works offline, making it perfect for studying on the go.

BioRender

Purpose: Helps create professional biological illustrations for research, projects, and presentations.

Features:

  • Thousands of pre-made images (e.g., cells, proteins) to use in diagrams.
  • Easy to use for beginners and experts.
  • Paid app, but offers a free trial and discounts for schools.

Genome Compiler

Purpose: For designing and simulating DNA sequences.

Features:

  • Allows you to create genetic codes and predict their behavior.
  • Especially useful for genetic engineering.
  • Free for school use; advanced features require a paid version.

VMD (Visual Molecular Dynamics)

Purpose: Visualize molecules and proteins in 3D.

Features:

  • Lets you see molecules at an atomic level.
  • Helps study molecular interactions and movements.
  • Free to use and ideal for educational purposes.
